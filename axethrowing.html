To briefly summarize my video, I used a game that I created in Unreal Engine 4.23.1 that would be a character throwing an Axe at a dummy model. Once the dummy model is hit, it falls to the ground and the Axe is stuck on the dummy model’s body. The process starts with the Axe being aimed at the dummy model and then thrown which exerts a force on the Axe which makes it gain momentum. When the Axe is thrown the momentum from the character’s arm is transferred to the Axe itself and when the Axe collides with the dummy model, the Axe stops moving, and the dummy model falls down, which means all the momentum is transferred. This would mean that the conservation of momentum as my model follows Newton’s 3rd Law and the system’s total momentum before the collision is equal to the total momentum after the collision.

Disclaimer: The project is more of a demonstration of my physics knowledge combined with my game development. This game is not particularly polished and does have some bugs.
